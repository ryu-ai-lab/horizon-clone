## [Anthropic's Claude Implements Identity Verification](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/14328960-identity-verification-on-claude) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Anthropic, the developer of the AI model Claude, has implemented a new identity verification requirement for its users. This policy mandates users to verify their identity, sparking significant debate within the community regarding its implications. This policy change by a major AI provider has profound implications for user data privacy, potentially restricting access for international users, and raising questions about the fairness and neutrality of access to advanced AI services. It highlights a growing trend in the AI industry towards stricter user authentication and compliance. While Anthropic states it won't use identity data for model training, the third-party verification vendor, Persona, can use the data to improve fraud prevention. Users who fail the verification process may be permanently locked out without a retry option, and some community members note that this policy has been in place for several months, not just recently introduced.

hackernews · bathory · 6월21일 12:44 · [커뮤니티 토론](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48618455)

**배경 지식**: Identity verification is a process used to confirm an individual's identity, often involving government-issued IDs, to ensure compliance with regulations, prevent fraud, or restrict access based on age or location. In the context of AI, it can be implemented to prevent misuse of powerful models, adhere to "know your customer" (KYC) principles, or comply with export controls.

**커뮤니티 토론**: The community expresses significant concerns regarding data privacy, particularly the sharing of identity data with third-party vendors like Persona for fraud prevention training. Many international users fear being locked out of advanced models due to geopolitical restrictions, while others criticize the lack of transparency and the permanent lockout for failed verifications, drawing parallels to net neutrality and questioning AI neutrality. Some also point out that this policy has existed for months, not just recently.
